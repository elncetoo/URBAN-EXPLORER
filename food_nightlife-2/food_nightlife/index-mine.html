Copenhagen Street Food

Copenhagen Street Food on Papirøen is the city's first and only genuine street food market. In the small food trucks you can get delicious sustainable street food from all corners of the world. It is also a place for creative souls, such as artists and musicians, and there are often events in between meals.

Restaurant Bror

Bror is the best place in Copenhagen to satisfy your cravings for Danish food, fish dishes, even vegeterian meals. Their beer selection is very affordable, not to mention stronger than rocket fuel. Dinner will set you back about €40, which is pretty reasonable for Copenhagen.

Floss bar

Once the scene in Copenhagen for 80s punk-rockers, Floss is now a dive bar that still retains its original debaucherous charm. Floss offers cheap drinks, billiard tables and rock music. The weekends bring two Guld Tuborgs for 27 DKK during happy hour from 22-23, and ten tequila shots are always 100 DKK. Those feeling brave can select between two Absinthe options. Smoking is permitted inside.

Jazz House

Situated right in the heart of Copenhagen, Jazzhouse offers a contemporary and cutting edge take on jazz and bordering genres on two stages.Jazzhouse is a place for experiments, constantly exploring the shapes of jazz to come, presenting underground artists as well as defining musical legends. Jazzhouse sees jazz as an open form of expression rather than a genre with a fixed set of rules.

Culture box

Culture Box is not only the most prominent venue for electronic music in Danmark, but also a government supported regional music venue, chosen to be the main platform for the electronic scene in the country. Culture Box is used for a wide range of events, including everything from teaching sessions to dance shows and movie productions. Durring the weekends, the club always has a solid line up of the best international and local artists of the underground music scene. The music styles are broad, but ranges from electronica, through bass music, to house and techno.
